## Global Education Inequality Dashboard
Measuring the Gap : A Data-Driven Analysis of Global Education Inequality


Analysed UNESCO + World Bank data across 190+ countries (2000–2023) to uncover patterns in literacy rates, gender gaps, enrollment, and government spending on education.

**Key Findings:**
- **Global Crisis Pockets:** Over 10 countries globally still face an acute primary education crisis, with each individual nation having over 1 million children completely out of school.
- **Socioeconomic Divide:** The absolute volume of out-of-school primary children is heavily concentrated in low-income brackets, with Sub-Saharan Africa showing the highest structural exclusion totals.
- **Gender Disparity Gaps:** While the global average sits closer to parity, top-tier regional disparities reveal extreme baseline deviations from the perfect 1.0 Gender Parity Index (GPI) target in the most vulnerable geographic zones.
